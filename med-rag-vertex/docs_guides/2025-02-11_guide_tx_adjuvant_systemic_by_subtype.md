## Treatment guidance (2025-02-11) · Selecting adjuvant systemic therapy in early breast cancer by receptor subtype

Scope: resected stage I–III invasive carcinoma, post-operative setting, decision about which
*class* of systemic therapy to offer. This note does not cover drug-level choices within a
class, nor the neoadjuvant setting.

**Step one is the receptor panel, not the tumour size.** ER, PR and HER2 must be reported on
adequate tissue before any systemic plan is drafted. If the core needle biopsy result is
equivocal, borderline, or based on scant tissue, repeat testing on the surgical specimen
rather than proceeding on a provisional subtype.

**Hormone-receptor-positive, HER2-negative.** Endocrine therapy is the backbone for nearly
every patient in this group; the real question is whether chemotherapy adds anything on top.
For node-negative tumours roughly 0.6–5.0 cm, a validated multigene recurrence assay is the
usual tie-breaker: a low genomic score generally supports endocrine therapy alone, a high
score supports adding chemotherapy. In premenopausal patients under 50 with an intermediate
score, apparent chemotherapy benefit may partly reflect chemotherapy-induced ovarian
suppression, which changes how the result should be read. With 1–3 involved nodes the assay
is informative but less decisive and the case belongs in tumour board discussion.

**ER-low disease (1–10% staining)** should not be managed as if it were robustly
hormone-driven. It behaves closer to triple-negative disease, and endocrine therapy alone is
rarely sufficient.

**HER2-positive, any hormone-receptor status.** Chemotherapy plus HER2-directed antibody
therapy is the expected backbone; regimen selection is addressed separately.

**Triple-negative.** There is no endocrine or HER2 target, so cytotoxic chemotherapy —
typically an anthracycline and taxane sequence — is the only systemic option. For tumours
2 cm or larger, or with clinically involved nodes, delivering that chemotherapy before
surgery is usually preferred so that response is measurable. Germline BRCA1/2 testing should
be arranged early, since a pathogenic variant opens an additional adjuvant option.

**Sequencing.** Chemotherapy precedes endocrine therapy; tamoxifen is not given concurrently
with cytotoxic chemotherapy. Radiotherapy planning proceeds in parallel and is not delayed
waiting for genomic results.

Treatment decisions belong to the multidisciplinary team and the patient; decision-support
tools do not prescribe.
