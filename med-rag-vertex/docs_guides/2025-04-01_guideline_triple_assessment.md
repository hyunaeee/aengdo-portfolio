## Guideline summary (2025-04) · Evaluation of a palpable breast mass

1. Triple assessment is the standard for any discrete palpable mass:
   clinical examination + imaging + tissue sampling when indicated.
2. Imaging by age: ultrasound first if under ~30; diagnostic mammography
   plus ultrasound if 30 or older.
3. BI-RADS governs next steps: 1-2 routine care; 3 short-interval follow-up
   (~6 months); 4-5 tissue diagnosis (core needle biopsy preferred over FNA).
4. Red flags mandating urgent workup regardless of age: hard fixed mass,
   skin dimpling or peau d'orange, nipple retraction, unilateral single-duct
   bloody discharge, suspicious axillary nodes.
5. Cyclical bilateral pain without focal findings is not an indication for
   biopsy; reassure with explicit safety-netting.
6. Document family history; refer for genetic counseling when criteria met
   (e.g., young-onset or multiple affected relatives).
