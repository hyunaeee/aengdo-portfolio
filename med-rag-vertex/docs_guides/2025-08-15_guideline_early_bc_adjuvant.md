## Guideline summary (2025-08) · Adjuvant therapy for early breast cancer

1. Adjuvant systemic therapy is selected by receptor status (ER/PR, HER2),
   nodal status, tumor size/grade, and menopausal status.
2. HER2-positive early disease: chemotherapy plus one year of
   trastuzumab-based HER2-targeted therapy is the standard backbone;
   regimen details are individualized by the oncology team.
3. Hormone-receptor-positive disease: endocrine therapy —
   tamoxifen for premenopausal patients (with or without ovarian
   suppression); aromatase inhibitors for postmenopausal patients.
   Menopausal and receptor status are REQUIRED before choosing between them.
4. Radiotherapy follows breast-conserving surgery as standard.
5. Recurrence (local, regional, or distant) requires biopsy confirmation
   and full restaging before any change of systemic therapy.
6. All treatment decisions belong to the multidisciplinary team and the
   patient; decision-support tools do not prescribe.
