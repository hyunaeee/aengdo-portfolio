## Case record 2025-02-11 · 22F · mobile solid mass, right breast

**Presentation.** A 22-year-old nulliparous woman noticed a firm, painless lump in the
right breast, upper outer quadrant, while showering. She reported it had been present for
roughly four months and did not change with her menstrual cycle. No nipple discharge, no
skin dimpling, no prior breast surgery, no radiation exposure. No first- or second-degree
family history of breast or ovarian cancer.

**Examination.** A single 2.1 cm mass, smooth and rubbery, freely mobile within the
breast tissue ("slippery" on palpation — 잘 밀리는 종괴). Overlying skin normal. Nipple
normal, no retraction. No palpable axillary or supraclavicular lymphadenopathy.

**Imaging.** Ultrasound alone was appropriate for her age; mammography was not performed
because of dense fibroglandular tissue and low pretest probability of malignancy. Targeted
ultrasound showed an oval, circumscribed, wider-than-tall (parallel orientation) mass
measuring 21 × 14 × 9 mm, homogeneously hypoechoic, with mild posterior acoustic
enhancement and no internal vascularity on colour Doppler. No calcifications, no
architectural distortion. Assessment: **BI-RADS 3, probably benign fibroadenoma.**

**Features pointing away from malignancy.** Age under 25; a mobile mass rather than one
fixed to surrounding tissue; oval shape with circumscribed margins; parallel orientation;
absence of posterior shadowing, spiculation, or duct extension; no axillary nodes.

**Red flags that would have changed the plan.** Any of the following would have moved this
from surveillance to immediate core needle biopsy: interval growth greater than about 20%
across follow-up intervals (raising phyllodes tumour), size above 3 cm at presentation,
non-parallel orientation or angular/microlobulated margins, posterior acoustic shadowing,
fixation to skin or chest wall, associated bloody nipple discharge, or a known pathogenic
BRCA variant in the family.

**Course.** Short-interval surveillance ultrasound at 6, 12 and 24 months. The mass
measured 20 × 14 mm at each visit — stable. No biopsy was performed. She was offered
excision for symptom or anxiety reasons and declined. Discharged to routine care at 24
months with safety-netting instructions.

Teaching point: a mobile, oval, circumscribed, parallel-oriented solid mass in a woman
under 25 is characteristic of fibroadenoma and may be followed with a BI-RADS 3 imaging
surveillance protocol rather than biopsied — but documented interval growth is the finding
that breaks the algorithm.
