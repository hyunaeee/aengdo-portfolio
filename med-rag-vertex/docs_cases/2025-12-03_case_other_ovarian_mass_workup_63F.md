## Case record 2025-12-03 · 63F · complex adnexal mass and ascites — OVARIAN, not breast

**Presentation.** A 63-year-old postmenopausal woman, para 2, presented with four
months of abdominal bloating, early satiety, and a 5 kg weight loss. She had been
treated for presumed irritable bowel syndrome. Examination found a distended
abdomen with shifting dullness and a fixed mass palpable on bimanual pelvic
examination. Clinical breast examination was normal and her screening mammogram
eleven months earlier was BI-RADS 1 — this record documents **no breast disease**.

**Imaging and markers.** Transvaginal ultrasound showed a 9 cm right adnexal mass
with thick septations, solid mural nodules, and marked internal vascularity, plus
moderate ascites — O-RADS 5. Contrast CT of the chest, abdomen, and pelvis showed
omental caking, peritoneal implants, and bulky retroperitoneal nodes; there was no
parenchymal liver metastasis. CA-125 was 1,480 U/mL with CEA 2.1 ng/mL, and the
CA-125:CEA ratio above 25 argued against a metastatic gastrointestinal primary.

**Pathology and genetics.** Image-guided omental core biopsy showed high-grade
serous carcinoma, WT1 positive, p53 aberrant (null pattern), PAX8 positive —
a Müllerian primary. **ER weak positivity was noted immunohistochemically but is
not used to direct endocrine therapy here**, and HER2 testing was not part of the
workup. Germline and somatic testing identified a pathogenic BRCA1 variant, which
matters both for PARP inhibitor maintenance and for cascade family testing;
elevated lifetime breast cancer risk was discussed as a future screening issue,
not as the current diagnosis.

**Management.** Given extensive peritoneal disease and poor predicted resectability,
the gynaecologic oncology tumour board recommended neoadjuvant carboplatin and
paclitaxel for three cycles, followed by interval debulking surgery aiming for no
gross residual disease, then three further cycles and PARP inhibitor maintenance.
There was no role for sentinel lymph node biopsy or for any breast-directed
intervention. Response was tracked by CA-125 trend and interval CT.

**Teaching point:** bloating, early satiety, ascites, and an O-RADS 5 adnexal mass
with a high CA-125 define an ovarian cancer pathway — neoadjuvant platinum-taxane
chemotherapy and interval debulking, with BRCA1 status driving maintenance — and
the incidental ER positivity in this tumour is a lexical trap, not a reason to
retrieve this case for a hormone-receptor breast cancer question.
