## Case record 2025-08-26 · 39F · 1.4 cm node-negative HER2-positive carcinoma, upfront surgery

**Presentation.** A 39-year-old premenopausal woman, fourteen months after weaning her second
child, felt a firm painless nodule in the right breast during self-examination. Cycles were
regular, there was no nipple discharge or skin change, no family history, and she had never
had prior breast imaging.

**Workup.** Ultrasound was the first-line study given her age: a 1.4 cm irregular hypoechoic
mass at the 10 o'clock position, 4 cm from the nipple, non-parallel in orientation with
posterior acoustic shadowing. Mammography in heterogeneously dense tissue confirmed an
irregular mass with a few associated coarse heterogeneous calcifications. Assessment was
BI-RADS 4C. Dedicated axillary ultrasound showed nodes with preserved fatty hila and cortical
thickness under 2 mm, so no node sampling was performed. Ultrasound-guided core biopsy of the
mass was taken with clip placement. Staging imaging beyond the breast was not indicated for
clinical stage I disease.

**Pathology.** Invasive ductal carcinoma, grade 3, ER <1% (negative), PR 0%, HER2 IHC 3+
(reflex ISH not required), Ki-67 45%, with adjacent focal high-grade DCIS.

**Course.** With a clinically node-negative sub-2 cm tumour, upfront surgery was chosen over
neoadjuvant systemic therapy — the tumour was already small enough that preoperative
treatment would not alter the surgical plan, and definitive pathological staging was
preferred. Breast-conserving surgery with sentinel node biopsy yielded a 15 mm invasive
carcinoma, closest margin 4 mm, 0/3 sentinel nodes negative — pT1c pN0. Baseline
echocardiography showed an LVEF of 62%. Adjuvant therapy was de-escalated to weekly
paclitaxel for twelve weeks with trastuzumab, continuing trastuzumab to complete one year,
in place of an anthracycline-containing regimen. Whole-breast radiotherapy with a tumour-bed
boost followed chemotherapy. Cardiac function was reassessed every three months. Fertility
counselling preceded chemotherapy and she banked oocytes. No endocrine therapy was given, as
the tumour was hormone-receptor negative.

**Teaching point.** Small node-negative HER2-positive disease is handled very differently from
bulky or node-positive HER2-positive disease: surgery comes first, and a single-agent taxane
with trastuzumab suffices — dual HER2 blockade, anthracyclines, and neoadjuvant sequencing
are reserved for higher-stage presentations.
