## Case record 2025-08-23 · 39M · enlarging deep mass of the upper arm — SARCOMA, not carcinoma

**Presentation.** A 39-year-old man reported a painless mass in the posterior
right upper arm, first noticed four months earlier and clearly enlarging. He
attributed it to a gym injury. On examination there was a firm, deep, immobile
mass of roughly 8 cm within the triceps compartment, without overlying skin
change or ulceration. No axillary lymphadenopathy. Chest wall and both breasts
were normal on examination; nothing in this record concerns breast tissue.

**Imaging.** MRI with contrast showed a 8.3 × 6.1 cm heterogeneous deep
intramuscular mass with irregular enhancement, central necrosis, and displacement
rather than encasement of the neurovascular bundle. Any soft-tissue mass that is
deep to fascia, larger than 5 cm, or enlarging should be treated as sarcoma until
proven otherwise. Staging CT of the chest was obtained because sarcoma metastasis
is characteristically **haematogenous to lung**, not lymphatic — it showed no
pulmonary nodules.

**Pathology.** Image-guided core needle biopsy along a planned longitudinal tract,
sited so the tract could be excised at definitive surgery, showed a high-grade
pleomorphic spindle cell neoplasm, FNCLCC grade 3, mitotically active with
necrosis. Immunohistochemistry was negative for cytokeratin, S100, desmin, and
CD34, supporting undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma. ER, PR, and HER2 were not
assessed; these markers are irrelevant to sarcoma biology and carry no
therapeutic implication here.

**Management.** The sarcoma multidisciplinary team recommended preoperative
(neoadjuvant) external beam radiotherapy followed by limb-sparing wide local
excision with negative margins. Anthracycline-based systemic therapy was
discussed for a large, deep, grade 3 tumour, with the discussion framed around
metastatic risk to lung rather than nodal spread. **Sentinel lymph node biopsy
was explicitly not performed** — regional nodal sampling has essentially no role
in this histology. Surveillance combined limb MRI and chest CT for local
recurrence and pulmonary metastasis.

**Teaching point:** a deep, enlarging, greater-than-5 cm extremity mass is a
sarcoma pathway — planned-tract core biopsy at a sarcoma centre, neoadjuvant
radiotherapy, wide excision, and lung-directed surveillance — and answering with
sentinel node biopsy, receptor-directed endocrine therapy, or breast adjuvant
regimens signals that the wrong document was retrieved.
