# SYNTHETIC CASE — for demo purposes only, not a real patient

## Case record 2025-03-14 · 62F · palpable breast lump

Presentation: 62-year-old woman, self-detected palpable lump in the right
breast, upper outer quadrant. No pain, no nipple discharge, no skin change.
No family history of breast cancer. Postmenopausal.

Workup: Diagnostic mammography showed an irregular, spiculated mass, 1.8 cm,
assessed BI-RADS 4C. Targeted ultrasound confirmed a hypoechoic mass with
angular margins. Ultrasound-guided core needle biopsy performed.

Pathology: Invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC), ER+ PR+ HER2-, Ki-67 15%.

Course: Breast-conserving surgery with sentinel lymph node biopsy. SLNB
negative (0/3). Adjuvant radiotherapy followed by endocrine therapy
(aromatase inhibitor, postmenopausal).

Teaching point: painless spiculated mass in a postmenopausal woman is
malignant until proven otherwise; BI-RADS 4C warrants tissue diagnosis
without delay.
