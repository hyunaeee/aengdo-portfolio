# SYNTHETIC CASE — for demo purposes only, not a real patient

## Case record 2025-11-05 · 45F · breast lump with skin dimpling

Presentation: 45-year-old woman with a 2-month history of a left breast lump
and new skin dimpling over it. Premenopausal. Mother diagnosed with breast
cancer at 60.

Workup: Mammography — 2.6 cm irregular mass with architectural distortion,
BI-RADS 5. Ultrasound — hypoechoic mass invading superficial fascia;
two abnormal axillary nodes. Core biopsy of mass and node.

Pathology: Invasive ductal carcinoma, ER- PR- HER2+, node-positive.

Course: Staging complete; neoadjuvant chemotherapy plus HER2-targeted
therapy (trastuzumab-based) before surgery, per multidisciplinary board.

Teaching point: skin dimpling (tethering of Cooper's ligaments) is a
red-flag sign strongly associated with invasive carcinoma; BI-RADS 5 with
suspicious nodes calls for biopsy of both mass and node and neoadjuvant
discussion — differential still includes fat necrosis and prior-surgery
scarring, resolved only by tissue.
