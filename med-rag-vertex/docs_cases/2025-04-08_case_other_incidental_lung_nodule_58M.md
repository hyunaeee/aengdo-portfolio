## Case record 2025-04-08 · 58M · incidental pulmonary nodule on CT — LUNG, not breast

**Presentation.** A 58-year-old man, 30 pack-year former smoker, underwent
contrast CT of the abdomen for right-sided renal colic. The lower cuts of the
study incidentally captured the lung bases and showed a nodule in the right lower
lobe. He was entirely asymptomatic: no cough, no haemoptysis, no weight loss, no
chest wall pain. He is male, has no palpable chest wall or axillary mass, and
this record contains **no breast pathology of any kind**.

**Imaging.** Dedicated thin-section chest CT characterised an 11 mm part-solid
nodule with a 6 mm solid component and mild spiculation in the right lower lobe
— Lung-RADS 4B. No cavitation, no calcification. Comparison with a chest
radiograph from 2021 suggested the lesion was new. Mediastinal and hilar nodes
measured under 1 cm on short axis. FDG PET-CT showed focal uptake in the nodule
(SUVmax 4.1) with no hypermetabolic mediastinal nodes and no evidence of distant
metastasis. Brain MRI was negative.

**Pathology.** CT-guided percutaneous core biopsy yielded invasive adenocarcinoma,
TTF-1 positive, p40 negative — a lung primary rather than a metastasis from
elsewhere. Molecular testing found no EGFR, ALK, or ROS1 alteration; PD-L1 tumour
proportion score was 15%. Hormone receptor immunohistochemistry was not performed
and would be clinically meaningless in this context.

**Management.** Endobronchial ultrasound nodal staging was negative, giving
clinical stage IA2 (cT1bN0M0) non-small cell lung cancer. He underwent
video-assisted thoracoscopic right lower lobectomy with systematic mediastinal
lymph node sampling; all 14 sampled nodes were negative, confirming pathologic
stage IA2 with negative bronchial margins. Adjuvant chemotherapy was **not**
recommended for a completely resected node-negative stage IA tumour. Surveillance
was set at low-dose CT every six months for two years, then annually, with smoking
cessation counselling reinforced.

**Teaching point:** an incidentally detected pulmonary nodule is staged with
Lung-RADS, PET-CT, and invasive mediastinal nodal assessment, and treated with
anatomic lobectomy — a retriever that answers a breast imaging or breast staging
question with this lung case has confused shared words (nodule, biopsy, node,
staging, adjuvant chemotherapy) for shared disease.
