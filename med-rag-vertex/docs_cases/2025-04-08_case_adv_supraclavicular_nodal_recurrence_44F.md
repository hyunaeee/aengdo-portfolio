## Case record 2025-04-08 · 44F · supraclavicular and internal mammary nodal recurrence, 20 months after HER2-directed therapy

**Presentation.** A 44-year-old premenopausal woman presented with three weeks of painless
fullness in the left supraclavicular fossa and new heaviness of the left arm. Twenty months
earlier she had completed treatment for left-sided HER2-positive breast cancer: neoadjuvant
dose-dense doxorubicin/cyclophosphamide followed by paclitaxel with trastuzumab and pertuzumab,
then left mastectomy with axillary dissection showing residual disease (ypT2 ypN2a), followed by
14 cycles of adjuvant T-DM1 and post-mastectomy radiotherapy to the chest wall and axilla. The
original tumor was ER 0%, PR 0%, HER2 IHC 3+.

**Restaging workup.** Ultrasound showed a 2.4 cm rounded left supraclavicular node with absent
fatty hilum, plus two smaller level IV nodes. FDG PET/CT demonstrated avid left supraclavicular
and left internal mammary nodes, with no pulmonary, hepatic, or skeletal uptake. Contrast MRI of
the brain was performed because of the HER2-positive phenotype and was negative. Echocardiography
showed LVEF 58%.

**Biopsy confirmation.** Ultrasound-guided core biopsy of the supraclavicular node returned
metastatic carcinoma of breast origin, ER 0%, PR 0%, HER2 IHC 3+ — concordant with the primary
and confirming persistent HER2-driven biology despite prior trastuzumab, pertuzumab, and T-DM1.

**Management decision.** Because the internal mammary and supraclavicular basins are regional,
this was staged as recurrent locoregional (stage IIIC-equivalent) disease rather than M1, and the
board pursued aggressive multimodality control. Given relapse on adjuvant T-DM1, the disease was
considered T-DM1-refractory, and systemic therapy began with trastuzumab deruxtecan. Radiation
oncology reviewed the prior treatment plan and dose-volume histograms; the supraclavicular fossa
lay at the field edge, so re-irradiation was judged feasible after maximal systemic response, with
surgical nodal clearance reserved for residual disease. Ovarian function preservation counseling
and a repeat germline panel were arranged.

**Teaching point.** This differs from a first presentation in that the relapse interval itself is
the key datum: recurrence 20 months after completing HER2-directed therapy defines
treatment-refractory biology and moves the patient past first-line anti-HER2 options. It also
differs from an isolated axillary recurrence — supraclavicular and internal mammary involvement
demands re-irradiation planning against a prior dose map rather than straightforward dissection.
