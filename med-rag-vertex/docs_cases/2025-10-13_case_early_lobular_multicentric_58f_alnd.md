## Case record 2025-10-13 · 58F · mammographically occult invasive lobular carcinoma, node-positive

**Presentation.** A 58-year-old postmenopausal woman described a persistent area of altered
texture in the upper outer left breast over about five months, without a lump she could
outline and without skin or nipple change. She had used menopausal hormone therapy for four
years, stopping at age 55. Examination found a firm, ill-defined region of thickening roughly
4 cm across with no palpable mass edge and no axillary node palpable.

**Workup.** Mammography showed extremely dense tissue with a subtle developing asymmetry in
the left upper outer quadrant and no calcifications; the study was initially assessed BI-RADS 0
pending ultrasound. Ultrasound found a 2.1 cm ill-defined hypoechoic area with marked
posterior shadowing, poorly separable from surrounding parenchyma, plus two level I axillary
nodes with cortical thickening to 5 mm and loss of the fatty hilum — assessed BI-RADS 5.
Core biopsy of the breast lesion and fine-needle aspiration of a node were performed. Breast
MRI was obtained because clinical extent exceeded the sonographic finding, and showed 5.8 cm
of non-mass enhancement with two satellite foci in separate quadrants.

**Pathology.** Invasive lobular carcinoma, classic type, with single-file infiltration and
absent membranous E-cadherin on immunohistochemistry. Grade 2, ER 100%, PR 70%, HER2 IHC 0,
Ki-67 12%. Node aspirate positive for carcinoma.

**Course.** Multicentric disease across quadrants excluded breast conservation, and total
mastectomy was performed. Sentinel mapping retrieved four nodes, three containing
macrometastases, so completion axillary lymph node dissection followed; two of a further
twelve nodes were positive, giving five positive nodes overall. The largest invasive focus
measured 3.4 cm — pT2 pN2a. Genomic recurrence assays were not pursued given the nodal
burden. She received adjuvant anthracycline followed by taxane chemotherapy, then
post-mastectomy radiotherapy to the chest wall with supraclavicular and internal mammary
coverage, then letrozole with extended endocrine therapy planned beyond five years.

**Teaching point.** Invasive lobular carcinoma consistently under-declares its size on
mammography and ultrasound; when clinical extent outruns the imaging, preoperative MRI
changes the operation — here from attempted conservation to mastectomy — and lobular
histology should lower the threshold for obtaining it.
