# SYNTHETIC CASE — for demo purposes only, not a real patient

## Case record 2025-09-10 · 55F · axillary lump 3 years after BCS

Presentation: 55-year-old woman, 3 years after breast-conserving surgery and
radiotherapy for left IDC (ER+ HER2-, pT1cN0), on tamoxifen. New firm lump
in the left axilla, 1.5 cm, non-tender.

Workup: Ultrasound showed a suspicious lymph node with cortical thickening
and loss of fatty hilum. Core biopsy confirmed carcinoma consistent with
breast primary — regional recurrence. Restaging CT chest/abdomen and bone
scan negative for distant metastasis.

Course: Axillary lymph node dissection, switch of endocrine therapy to an
aromatase inhibitor after ovarian function assessment, regional radiotherapy
review by multidisciplinary tumor board.

Teaching point: a new axillary mass in a breast-cancer survivor is regional
recurrence until proven otherwise; tissue confirmation and full restaging
precede any treatment change. This is a DIFFERENT clinical situation from a
first-presentation breast lump, even at similar age.
