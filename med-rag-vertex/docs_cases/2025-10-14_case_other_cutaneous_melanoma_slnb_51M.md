## Case record 2025-10-14 · 51M · pigmented back lesion with sentinel node biopsy — MELANOMA, not breast

**Presentation.** A 51-year-old man with fair skin and a history of blistering
sunburns was referred after his partner noticed a changing mole on the left upper
back. Over eight months it had darkened, developed an irregular notched border,
and intermittently bled after towel drying. Dermoscopy showed an atypical
pigment network with blue-white veil and irregular globules. Examination of the
chest wall and both axillae was otherwise normal; there is **no breast lesion**
in this record, only a cutaneous one.

**Pathology.** Excisional biopsy with 2 mm margins showed superficial spreading
melanoma, Breslow thickness 2.3 mm, no ulceration, mitotic rate 3/mm², clear
peripheral and deep margins on the diagnostic excision. Staging was pT3a.
Immunohistochemistry used SOX10 and Melan-A; **ER, PR, and HER2 were not tested**
and have no role in melanoma. BRAF V600E mutation testing was positive, which is
what actually determines targeted systemic options here.

**Nodal staging.** Because Breslow thickness exceeded 1 mm, lymphoscintigraphy
and sentinel lymph node biopsy were offered. Technetium-99m colloid injected
intradermally around the biopsy scar mapped to the **left axilla**, and two
sentinel nodes were harvested. One node contained a 0.4 mm subcapsular deposit of
melanoma. Note that a positive axillary sentinel node here reflects drainage from
the back skin, not from breast parenchyma — the anatomy is shared, the disease is
not. Completion axillary dissection was **not** performed; current practice
favours nodal ultrasound surveillance after a positive sentinel node in melanoma.

**Management.** Wide local excision of the primary site was completed with 2 cm
margins. Staging CT and brain MRI showed no distant metastasis, giving stage IIIA
disease. Adjuvant anti-PD-1 immunotherapy was recommended for one year; cytotoxic
adjuvant chemotherapy was **not** offered, as it is ineffective in this setting.
Follow-up included skin surveillance every three months and axillary ultrasound.

**Teaching point:** sentinel lymph node biopsy draining to the axilla is not
unique to breast cancer — in cutaneous melanoma it follows Breslow thickness,
completion dissection is avoided, and adjuvant therapy is checkpoint blockade
guided by BRAF status, so retrieving this case for a breast SLNB question is a
clear error.
