## Case record 2025-10-16 · 39F · brain metastases in BRCA1-associated triple-negative breast cancer, 14 months after surgery

**Presentation.** A 39-year-old woman was brought to the emergency department after a witnessed
generalized tonic-clonic seizure, preceded by two weeks of morning-predominant headache and
intermittent nausea. Examination showed mild left pronator drift and a left facial droop; she was
otherwise alert and orientated. Fourteen months earlier she had completed treatment for
triple-negative breast cancer (ER 0%, PR 0%, HER2 IHC 0) with a germline BRCA1 pathogenic variant:
neoadjuvant carboplatin and paclitaxel followed by anthracycline-cyclophosphamide, bilateral
mastectomy with residual disease (ypT1c ypN1), adjuvant capecitabine, and post-mastectomy
radiotherapy.

**Restaging workup.** Non-contrast CT head showed a right frontal mass with surrounding low
attenuation. Contrast-enhanced brain MRI — the definitive study — demonstrated four enhancing
lesions: a 2.8 cm right frontal lesion with extensive vasogenic edema and 4 mm midline shift, two
subcentimeter parietal lesions, and a 6 mm cerebellar lesion. There was no leptomeningeal
enhancement. Staging CT of chest, abdomen, and pelvis showed two stable subcentimeter lung
nodules and no hepatic or skeletal disease, so the CNS was the dominant and near-isolated site of
progression.

**Biopsy confirmation.** She underwent craniotomy and gross total resection of the symptomatic
right frontal lesion. Histology confirmed metastatic carcinoma consistent with breast origin,
ER 0%, PR 0%, HER2 IHC 0 — concordant triple-negative phenotype, with no change in biologic class.

**Management decision.** Dexamethasone was given for edema and levetiracetam for seizure
prophylaxis after the index seizure. Following resection, stereotactic radiosurgery was delivered
to the resection cavity and to the three unresected lesions; whole-brain radiotherapy was
deliberately avoided to preserve neurocognitive function, given her age and limited lesion count.
Systemic therapy was switched to olaparib on the basis of the germline BRCA1 variant, with
sacituzumab govitecan held in reserve, and driving was suspended per local seizure regulations.

**Teaching point.** A first presentation is worked up from the breast outward; here the brain is a
sanctuary site whose disease can progress while extracranial disease stays controlled, so
systemic response elsewhere gave false reassurance. New neurological symptoms in a
triple-negative survivor require urgent contrast MRI rather than CT, and management is
local-therapy-first — resection or radiosurgery with steroid and seizure control — before systemic
therapy is reconsidered.
