## Case record 2025-05-02 · 31F · cyclical bilateral breast pain

Presentation: 31-year-old woman with bilateral breast pain worsening in the
week before menstruation, resolving after. No palpable mass on
self-examination. No discharge, no skin change. Anxious about breast cancer
after reading online.

Workup: Clinical breast examination unremarkable. Given age and cyclical
bilateral pattern, imaging deferred; reassurance and symptom diary advised.
Ultrasound offered for persistent focal symptoms — not required at this visit.

Assessment: Cyclical mastalgia, physiologic. No red-flag features
(no focal lump, no nipple discharge, no skin retraction, no axillary nodes).

Course: Symptom diary over two cycles confirmed cyclical pattern. Managed
conservatively (well-fitted bra, simple analgesia as needed). No imaging
abnormality on later routine screening.

Teaching point: bilateral cyclical mastalgia in a young woman without focal
findings is overwhelmingly benign; the priority is structured reassurance
and explicit safety-netting for red-flag changes.
