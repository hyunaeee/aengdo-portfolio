## Case record 2025-01-22 · 34F · rapidly enlarging left breast mass, triple-negative, germline BRCA1

**Presentation.** A 34-year-old premenopausal woman presented with a firm left breast mass
first noticed six weeks earlier, which she believed had roughly doubled in size. No nipple
discharge, no skin change, no constitutional symptoms. Nulliparous, on no hormonal
contraception. Family history: maternal grandmother with ovarian cancer at 52, maternal aunt
with breast cancer at 41.

**Workup.** Given her age, ultrasound was performed first and showed a 3.2 cm oval, largely
circumscribed hypoechoic mass at the 2 o'clock position with posterior acoustic enhancement
and a thin echogenic rim — features overlapping with fibroadenoma, but with disorganised
internal vascularity and an adjacent 1.6 cm level I axillary node showing cortical thickening.
Mammography in extremely dense tissue (ACR category D) demonstrated only a subtle focal
asymmetry. The combined assessment was BI-RADS 4C. Ultrasound-guided core needle biopsy of
the mass and fine-needle aspiration of the node were performed. Bilateral MRI showed a single
index lesion with no additional ipsilateral or contralateral disease. Staging CT and bone
scan were negative.

**Pathology.** Invasive ductal carcinoma, grade 3, pushing borders, brisk stromal
lymphocytic infiltrate. ER 0%, PR 0%, HER2 IHC score 0, Ki-67 70%. Node aspirate positive
for carcinoma. Clinical stage cT2 cN1 M0, triple-negative. Germline panel returned a
pathogenic BRCA1 variant.

**Course.** Neoadjuvant carboplatin–taxane followed by anthracycline–cyclophosphamide, with
pembrolizumab throughout. Response was partial; the node normalised on imaging. Given the
germline result she elected bilateral mastectomy with immediate implant reconstruction, with
post-neoadjuvant sentinel node biopsy using dual tracer. Final pathology showed a 9 mm
residual invasive focus with treatment effect and one of four sentinel nodes harbouring a
4 mm metastasis, so completion axillary dissection followed (0 of a further 11 nodes
positive). Because invasive residual disease remained, adjuvant olaparib was started
alongside completion of pembrolizumab, with post-mastectomy radiotherapy to the chest wall
and regional nodes. Risk-reducing salpingo-oophorectomy was deferred until after
radiotherapy.

**Teaching point.** A circumscribed, enhancement-positive mass in a woman under 40 is not
reassuring — BRCA1-associated triple-negative carcinoma characteristically mimics
fibroadenoma on ultrasound, and failure to reach a pathological complete response redirects
management toward escalated adjuvant therapy rather than observation.
