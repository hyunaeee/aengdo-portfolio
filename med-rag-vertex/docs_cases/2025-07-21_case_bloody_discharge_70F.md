# SYNTHETIC CASE — for demo purposes only, not a real patient

## Case record 2025-07-21 · 70F · unilateral bloody nipple discharge

Presentation: 70-year-old woman with spontaneous bloody discharge from a
single duct of the left nipple for 3 weeks. No palpable mass. No pain.

Workup: Mammography without discrete mass; retroareolar ultrasound showed a
5 mm intraductal lesion. Ductography localized a filling defect in a single
duct.

Pathology: Microdochectomy — intraductal papilloma with focal atypia; final
pathology excluded DCIS in the excised specimen.

Course: Surveillance imaging at 6 and 12 months, stable.

Teaching point: unilateral single-duct bloody discharge in an older patient
requires full workup even with no palpable mass — intraductal papilloma is
the most common cause, but DCIS must be excluded histologically.
